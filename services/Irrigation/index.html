What We See Most

Common Irrigation System Problems We Diagnose and Repair

Pump Won't Prime or Loses Prime

On an irrigation-dedicated pump, a loss of prime usually points to a foot valve, suction-side leak, or worn seal. Florida summers and seasonal cycling speed up the wear. We trace the issue back to the actual cause instead of just re-priming and hoping.

Pressure Drop and Failed Zones

When zones start running short, drying out spots in the lawn, or shutting off entirely, pressure is dropping somewhere in the system. The pump, pressure tank, or pressure switch is usually where the problem lives. We test the whole system before recommending a fix.

Motor and Control Box Failure

Irrigation pump motors and control boxes don't last forever, especially when they cycle dozens of times a day through Florida summers. Capacitors fail, motor windings burn out, and control box components corrode. We diagnose whether it's a component-level repair or a full motor replacement.

Equipment-Level Leaks

Drips and leaks at the pump housing, fittings, or pressure tank connections cost you water and pressure both. Our work covers the equipment itself: pump, tank, and the lines directly tied to the pressure system. In-house plumbing past the pressure tank is outside what we handle.

Pump Won't Stop Cycling

A pump that runs constantly or short-cycles every few seconds is usually telling you the pressure switch is bad, the bladder tank has lost air charge, or there's a leak somewhere keeping pressure from holding. Diagnosing which one matters, because the fix is different for each.

Post-Freeze and Seasonal Startup Failures

Tampa Bay catches the occasional hard freeze, and Ocala sees them more often. When you fire the irrigation system back up after a cold snap or after the off-season, cracked pump housings, blown pressure switches, and seized motors are common. We handle the diagnostic and the repair.
