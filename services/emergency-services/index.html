Diagnose the Symptom

When It's an Emergency: What to Look For

Most well emergencies are obvious when they happen. Some build slowly until something breaks. Here's what each one usually points to.

  • No water at all likely a pump failure or electrical issue No-Water Service Calls →
  • Weak or dropping pressure pressure tank, pressure switch, or pump No Pressure / Low Pressure →
  • Pump won't start or trips the breaker electrical failure at the motor, control box, or wiring Electrical Failures →
  • Visible tank damage ruptured bladder or waterlogged tank Ruptured Tank →
  • Water leaking from equipment pipe connections, fittings, or pump housing Plumbing Leaks →
  • Pump damaged by flooding submersible or above-ground water intrusion Flood Damage Pump Repair →
  • Pump failed and needs replacement can't wait for a repair window Emergency Pump Replacement →

If you're not sure which category fits, call. We can usually triage the situation over the phone.

No Pressure and Low Pressure

Low water pressure from a well pump has a few likely causes: a failing pressure tank, a waterlogged bladder, a worn pressure switch, or a pump losing output. We figure out which one it is and fix it.

Ruptured Tank

A ruptured or waterlogged pressure tank kills your water pressure and puts extra wear on the pump. If the pump is cycling on and off constantly, or pressure has dropped at every fixture in the house, the tank is usually where we look first.

Plumbing Leaks

We handle leaks at the equipment level: the pump housing, tank connections, pressure switch fittings, and the pipe coming off your well. If water is coming from somewhere in your pump system, we'll find it and stop it.

Flood Damage Pump Repair

Flooding damages well pumps in ways that aren't immediately visible. Submersible pumps take on debris; above-ground pumps lose motors and electrical components to water intrusion. Frequently Asked Questions

What counts as a well pump emergency? +

No water is the clearest sign. A pump that keeps tripping the breaker, a leaking pressure tank, or a pump that took on flood damage all count. If the system isn't working and you can't wait, that's enough.

Can a pump failure cause low water pressure throughout the whole house? +

Yes. A pump losing output, a waterlogged pressure tank, or a failed pressure switch can all drop pressure at every fixture. Low water pressure from a well pump usually comes down to one of those three things.

What should I do while waiting for a technician? +

If the pump's making unusual noises or tripping the breaker repeatedly, turn the breaker off. Running a failing pump can make things worse. If there's a visible leak at the tank or equipment, shut off the supply valve if you can reach it safely. Otherwise, leave the system alone and wait.
