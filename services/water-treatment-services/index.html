Water Treatment Services for Well Water in Tampa Bay and Ocala

Most homeowners on city water never think about what's in it. When you're on a private well, that's not an option.

Well water doesn't go through a municipal treatment plant before it reaches your tap. What comes up depends entirely on what's in the ground beneath your property — and in Tampa Bay and Ocala, that varies more than most people expect. Hard minerals, iron, sulfur, sediment, nitrates — the list is long, and no two wells are identical.

Boyette Pump & Well handles water treatment for residential and agricultural well systems across the Tampa Bay region and Ocala. If something's off with your water — the taste, the smell, the staining — we can figure out what it is and fix it.

What's in Your Well Water?

That depends on where you are and how deep your well goes.

Florida well water tends to run hard — high in calcium and magnesium, which builds up in pipes and appliances over time. Iron is common throughout the state. It shows up fast: orange staining on fixtures, a metallic taste, rust-colored laundry. Hydrogen sulfide produces that rotten egg smell. In agricultural areas around Ocala and parts of Tampa Bay, nitrate contamination is a real concern. It doesn't change the look or taste of the water, which is exactly what makes it worth testing for.

Sediment, bacteria, and other contaminants round out the picture. None of it is unusual. All of it is treatable.

Well water problems are specific to your property. A neighbor drawing from the same aquifer can have different results. Treatment starts with understanding what's actually in your water.

Water Treatment FAQs

How Do I Know Which Water Treatment System I Need?

A water test tells you. Staining, smell, and taste are useful clues, but they don't give you the full picture. Some problems — nitrates, for example — show no signs at all. We coordinate lab testing through a certified third party and use those results to make a specific recommendation.

How Long Does a Water Treatment System Last?

It depends on the system and how well it's maintained. Water softeners typically last 10–15 years with regular upkeep. Whole-house filtration systems vary — filter media needs periodic replacement, but the equipment itself can run much longer. RO membranes usually need replacement every 2–5 years depending on water quality and usage. Keeping up with service calls extends the life of any system.

What Is the Difference Between a Water Softener and a Water Filtration System?

A softener targets hardness — calcium and magnesium specifically. It doesn't filter contaminants, sediment, or bacteria. A filtration system addresses those issues but doesn't soften water. Many homes with well systems benefit from both, depending on what the water test shows.

How Often Do Water Treatment Systems Need to Be Serviced or Maintained?

Softeners need salt replenished regularly and a service check annually. Filtration systems need media or filter cartridge replacements on a schedule that depends on system type and water volume. RO systems need membrane checks every few years. We'll walk you through what your system needs at installation and can set up ongoing service from there.

Do You Service Existing Water Treatment Equipment or Only Install New Systems?

Both. If you have a softener, filtration system, or other treatment equipment that isn't performing, we can diagnose and service it. Installation isn't a requirement. If the equipment isn't worth repairing, we'll tell you that and go from there.
